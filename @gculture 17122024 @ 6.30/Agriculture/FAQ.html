Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Sustainable agriculture is the practice of farming that meets current agricultural needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own. It involves methods that preserve environmental health, economic profitability, and social and economic equity. These include crop rotation, organic pest control, water conservation, and renewable resources.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is a sustainable approach to managing pests by combining biological, cultural, physical, and chemical tools in a way that minimises economic, health, and environmental risks. My approach involves regular monitoring, identifying pests correctly, understanding their lifecycle, and using targeted management strategies that are effective, economical, and environmentally sound.

Technology plays a crucial role in modern agriculture. It enables farmers to increase efficiency and productivity through precision farming, genetically modified crops, and advanced machinery. Technology also helps monitor crop health and soil conditions, leading to better decision-making and sustainable practices.
